✏️ Part 2 — Question-Response (Q7–31)
25 questions. Hear a question or statement + three responses (A/B/C). No printed text. Choose the best response.
  • WH- questions — Who / What / Where / When / Why / How
  • Yes/No & Tag questions — confirmation requests
  • Statement responses — react to a remark or suggestion
💡 Key StrategyListen for the first word — it reveals the question type. Eliminate options that repeat words from the stimulus without answering logically.
🗣️ Part 3 — Conversations (Q32–70)
13 conversations, 3 questions each. Two or three speakers. Q65-70 include graphics (seating chart & market share pie chart).
  • Main idea / purpose
  • Factual detail — who/what/when/where
  • Inference & intention — implied meaning, quoted speech
  • Graphic — match audio to table or chart
💡 Key StrategyPre-read all 3 questions before the audio plays. For graphic questions, scan the table first. The first speaker turn usually identifies the situation.
🎤 Part 4 — Talks (Q71–100)
10 monologues, 3 questions each. One speaker. Q95-100 include graphics (auto repair price table & spa feedback survey).
  • Main purpose — What is the talk about?
  • Factual detail — specific info mentioned
  • Inference — Who is the speaker/audience?
  • Graphic — cross-reference audio with table
💡 Key StrategyThe opening sentence almost always states the main purpose. For intention questions (quoted speech), ask: "What is the speaker trying to accomplish?"
⚠️ Common Traps
Word repetition trap
A choice that repeats words from the audio is usually wrong — correct answers paraphrase.
Similar-sound trap
Watch for words that sound alike but mean different things ("acquisition" vs. "addition").
Scope trap
An answer may be true in general but not what was specifically asked.
Indirect answer
In Part 2, correct answers often imply yes/no through action rather than stating it directly.

🆕 Pure Vowels (Monophthongs)
/iː/ long "ee" — tense, lips spread wide
🔊 V-I-P /viː aɪ piː/ 🔊 energy /ˈen·ər·dʒiː/ 🔊 employee /ɪmˈplɔɪ·iː/ 🔊 catering /ˈkeɪt·ər·ɪŋ/
💡 Mouth shape: smile wide, tongue high at front. Hold the sound longer than /ɪ/.
/ɪ/ short "ih" — relaxed, tongue slightly high
🔊 innovative /ɪn·ə·veɪ·tɪv/ 🔊 immigration /ɪm·ɪ·ɡreɪ·ʃən/ 🔊 prestigious /pre·stɪ·dʒəs/ 🔊 pharmaceutical /fɑːr·mə·suː·tɪ·kl/
💡 Shorter & more relaxed than /iː/. The jaw drops slightly more. Common in unstressed syllables.
/e/ short "eh" — mid-front, like "bed"
🔊 energy /en·ər·dʒi/ 🔊 personnel /pɜːr·sə·nel/ 🔊 sanitary /ˈsæn·ɪ·ter·i/ 🔊 inspection /ɪn·spek·ʃən/
💡 Mid-front vowel. Jaw is open about halfway. Do NOT glide — it's a pure, flat sound.
/æ/ short "aa" — low-front, mouth wide open
🔊 adapt /æ·dæpt/ 🔊 pharma /fɑːr·mə/ 🔊 acquisition /æk·wɪ·zɪ·ʃən/ 🔊 catering /ˈkeɪt·ər·ɪŋ/
💡 Widest mouth opening. Drop your jaw fully, tongue flat at front. Often over-pronounced by Vietnamese learners.
/ʌ/ short "uh" — central, relaxed jaw
🔊 luncheon /lʌn·tʃən/ 🔊 bumper /bʌm·pər/ 🔊 entrepreneur /ˌɑːn·trə·prə·nɜːr/ 🔊 construction /kən·strʌk·ʃən/
💡 Central vowel — tongue in middle of mouth, jaw slightly open. Very common in stressed syllables spelled with "u".
/ɜː/ long "er" — mid-central, no lip rounding
🔊 personnel /pɜːr·sə·nel/ 🔊 entrepreneur /ˌɑːn·trə·prə·nɜːr/ 🔊 service /ˈsɜːr·vɪs/ 🔊 work /wɜːrk/
💡 Often spelled: "er", "ir", "ur", "ear" (in "learn"), "or" (in "word"). Tongue is central, held slightly longer.
/ɑː/ long "ah" — low-back, mouth open wide
🔊 spa /spɑː/ 🔊 pharma /fɑːr·mə/ 🔊 automotive /ɔː·tə·moʊ·tɪv/ 🔊 department /dɪ·pɑːrt·mənt/
💡 Mouth fully open, tongue pulled back and low. Common with "ar" and the vowel in "spa", "start", "car".
/ə/ schwa — unstressed, neutral, shortest vowel
🔊 acquisition /æk·wɪ·zɪ·ʃən/ 🔊 catering /ˈkeɪt·ər·ɪŋ/ 🔊 luncheon /ˈlʌntʃ·ən/ 🔊 evacuation /ɪ·væk·ju·eɪ·ʃən/
💡 The most common sound in English! Always in UNSTRESSED syllables. Completely neutral — no jaw movement. Vowel letter can be: a, e, i, o, u.
🆕 Diphthongs (Gliding Vowels)

A diphthong is two vowel sounds blended in one syllable — the tongue glides from one position to another. The first sound is always stronger.

/eɪ/ "ay" — glides from /e/ → /ɪ/
🔊 safety /ˈseɪf·ti/ 🔊 catering /ˈkeɪt·ər·ɪŋ/ 🔊 innovative /ˈɪn·ə·veɪ·tɪv/ 🔊 evacuation /ɪ·væk·ju·eɪ·ʃən/
💡 Glide: start at /e/ (like "bed") then slide tongue up to /ɪ/. Spelled: "a" (make), "ai" (wait), "ay" (say), "-ate" endings.
/aɪ/ "eye" — glides from /a/ → /ɪ/
🔊 side /saɪd/ 🔊 tire /taɪr/ 🔊 client /ˈklaɪ·ənt/ 🔊 identify /aɪ·ˈden·tɪ·faɪ/
💡 Glide: start low-front /a/ (big jaw drop), then slide up to /ɪ/. Spelled: "i" (time), "ie" (die), "igh" (night), "y" (my).
/ɔɪ/ "oy" — glides from /ɔː/ → /ɪ/
🔊 appointment /ə·ˈpɔɪnt·mənt/ 🔊 employee /ɪm·ˈplɔɪ·iː/ 🔊 invoice /ˈɪn·vɔɪs/ 🔊 avoid /ə·ˈvɔɪd/
💡 Glide: start with rounded lips /ɔː/ (like "law"), then slide to /ɪ/. Spelled: "oi" (point), "oy" (enjoy).
/aʊ/ "ow" — glides from /a/ → /ʊ/
🔊 outdoor /aʊt·dɔːr/ 🔊 south /saʊθ/ 🔊 downtown /ˈdaʊn·taʊn/ 🔊 account /ə·ˈkaʊnt/
💡 Glide: mouth opens wide /a/, then lips round and close towards /ʊ/. Spelled: "ou" (out), "ow" (town). Do NOT say /ɔː/.
/oʊ/ "oh" — glides from /o/ → /ʊ/
🔊 local /ˈloʊ·kəl/ 🔊 hotel /hoʊ·ˈtel/ 🔊 renovation /ˌren·ə·ˈveɪ·ʃən/ 🔊 proposal /prə·ˈpoʊ·zəl/
💡 Glide: start with mid-back /o/, then round lips into /ʊ/. Spelled: "o" (no), "oa" (coat), "ow" (know), "oe" (toe).
/ɪə/ "ear" — glides from /ɪ/ → /ə/
🔊 engineer /ˌen·dʒɪ·ˈnɪər/ 🔊 career /kə·ˈrɪər/ 🔊 idea /aɪ·ˈdɪə/ 🔊 experience /ɪk·ˈspɪər·ɪ·əns/
💡 Glide: start at /ɪ/ (relaxed high-front), then tongue drops to schwa /ə/. Spelled: "ear" (hear), "eer" (career), "ere" (here).
/eə/ "air" — glides from /e/ → /ə/
🔊 repair /rɪ·ˈpeər/ 🔊 aware /ə·ˈweər/ 🔊 there /ðeər/ 🔊 chairman /ˈtʃeər·mən/
💡 Glide: start at /e/ (like "bed"), then jaw relaxes to schwa. Spelled: "air" (fair), "are" (care), "ear" (wear), "ere" (where).
🆕 Minimal Pairs — Sound Discrimination

These word pairs differ by only one vowel sound. Listen carefully and click each word to compare.

/iː/ vs /ɪ/
/iː/ leave ↔ /ɪ/ live
/iː/ heat ↔ /ɪ/ hit
Tip: /iː/ is longer & more tense — feel the stretch in your lips!
/e/ vs /æ/
/e/ bed ↔ /æ/ bad
/e/ said ↔ /æ/ sad
Tip: /æ/ needs a bigger jaw drop — "bad" not "bed"!
/ʌ/ vs /ɑː/
/ʌ/ cut ↔ /ɑː/ cart
/ʌ/ luck ↔ /ɑː/ lark
Tip: /ɑː/ is longer, tongue pulled back. /ʌ/ is shorter, central tongue.
/eɪ/ vs /e/
/eɪ/ made ↔ /e/ med
/eɪ/ rate ↔ /e/ red
Tip: /eɪ/ has a glide (two sounds). /e/ is flat & pure (one sound).
/aɪ/ vs /eɪ/
/aɪ/ side ↔ /e/ said
🔊 client /aɪ/ ↔ 🔊 safety /eɪ/
Tip: /aɪ/ starts with jaw fully down. /eɪ/ starts mid — less drop.
/aʊ/ vs /oʊ/
/aʊ/ out ↔ /oʊ/ oat
/aʊ/ outdoor ↔ /oʊ/ hotel
Tip: /aʊ/ starts wide open then rounds. /oʊ/ stays more rounded throughout.
/ɪə/ vs /eə/
/ɪə/ ear ↔ /eə/ air
/ɪə/ engineer ↔ /eə/ repair
Tip: /ɪə/ — tongue HIGH then drops. /eə/ — tongue MID then drops.
