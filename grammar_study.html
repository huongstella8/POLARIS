🏗️ Sentence Components

Understanding the building blocks of every English sentence

Every English sentence is made of components. The most basic pattern is Subject + Verb, and from there we add objects, complements and adverbials.

🏛️ The Five Key Components

ComponentWhat it doesExample
SubjectWho/what the sentence is aboutMy arms are aching.
VerbThe action or stateSomething happened.
ObjectReceives the actionShe gave him a present.
ComplementDescribes the subject/objectShe seems tired.
AdverbialAdds context (when/where/how)He studies at school.
Direct & Indirect Objects

🎯 Two Types of Object

  • Direct object — what is given/done: She gave him a book.
  • Indirect object — to/for whom: She gave him a book.
We can also use VERB + direct object + to/for + indirect object:
→ She gave a book to him.  |  They found a chair for her.
Verbs that take two objects: give, send, offer, show, tell, teach, bring, buy, make, find, pick, reserve, win, earn

📍 Adverbials

Phrases and clauses that add context to a sentence

✦ Start Point

We can add adverbials to all the five main structures. They can be a single adverb, a prepositional phrase, or a clause.

📌 Adverbial Examples

My arms are acting terribly. → single adverb
Of course, this piano is heavy. → front position phrase
Fortunately, their house is nearby. → attitude adverbial
David actually bought Melania a present yesterday. → mid position

📐 Positions of Adverbials

  • Front position — before the subject: Of course, she was right.
  • Mid position — between subject and main verb (or after be/auxiliary): She always arrives early.
  • End position — after the verb/object: He ran very quickly down the street.
⚠️ Never put an adverbial between a verb and its direct object.
✗ She bought quickly the newspaper.
✓ She quickly bought the newspaper. / She bought the newspaper quickly.

🗂️ Types of Adverbials

TypeAnswersExamples
MannerHow?quickly, carefully, well, hard
PlaceWhere?at home, in the garden, here, abroad
TimeWhen?yesterday, in 2020, soon, at 5pm
FrequencyHow often?always, often, sometimes, rarely, never
DegreeHow much?very, quite, rather, extremely, a bit
AttitudeSpeaker's viewfortunately, honestly, obviously, clearly
💡 Thứ tự: Khi có nhiều trạng từ cuối câu, thứ tự thường là: Manner → Place → Time
She spoke quietly [manner] in the library [place] last night [time].

⚡ Adverbs

Position rules, types, and tricky adverbs: quite, already, yet, still, even, really

📐 Position Rules

  • Adverbs of manner go in end position: She sings beautifully.
  • Frequency adverbs go in mid position (before main verb, after be/auxiliary): She always comes late. / She is never late.
  • Definite time/frequency adverbs usually go at the end: I visit them every weekend.
  • Never between verb and direct object: ✗ She reads carefully books.
Mid-position order: Subject + (auxiliary) + adverb + main verb
→ They have always lived here. | He rarely complains.

🔍 Quite, Rather, Fairly, Pretty

  • quite = moderately: It's quite good.
  • quite + a/an + adj + noun: It's quite a good film. (NOT a quite good film)
  • rather suggests surprise or mild criticism: It's rather expensive.
  • fairly = moderately (neutral): The exam was fairly easy.

⏰ Already, Still, Yet, Just

AdverbPositionUseExample
alreadymid / endsooner than expectedShe's already left.
stillmidcontinuing situationHe's still sleeping.
yetendnegative/questionI haven't finished yet.
justmidvery recentlyShe has just arrived.

✨ Even & Really

  • even goes before the word it stresses: Even the teacher made a mistake.
  • even before a subject means "including this surprising person": Even Maria couldn't solve it.
  • really before an adjective = very: It's really difficult.
  • really before a verb = truly: I really don't understand.
  • really at the end = in reality (corrective): What did he say, really?
💡 Ghi nhớ: yet luôn ở cuối câu phủ định/câu hỏi | still ở giữa câu | already có thể ở giữa hoặc cuối câu khi nhấn mạnh sự ngạc nhiên.

📊 Gradable vs Non-gradable Adjectives

TypeModifiersExamples
Gradablevery, rather, quite, fairly, extremelybig, cold, happy, tired, difficult
Non-gradable (extreme)absolutely, completely, totally, utterlyenormous, freezing, exhausted, furious
Non-gradable (classifying)completely, totally (not very)wooden, nuclear, digital, unique

🔗 Adjective Patterns

  • Adj + to-infinitive: I'm happy to help. / It's difficult to understand.
  • Adj + -ing: It's worth reading. / She was busy cooking.
  • Adj + that-clause: I'm glad that you came. / She's aware that it's wrong.
  • Adj + preposition: afraid of, interested in, proud of, responsible for, different from

🔗 Linking Verbs + Adjective

be, seem, look, feel, taste, smell, sound, become, remain, appear, get, turn, stay, grow, prove, go, come
She looks tired. / The milk has gone sour. / He grew impatient.
💡 Sau linking verbs dùng adjective, KHÔNG dùng adverb: She looks beautiful (NOT beautifully).

📊 Comparison

Comparative, superlative, and other comparison structures

✦ Start Point

Accommodation would be cheaper in Athens.
I want to come home more relaxed and healthier.
It's the most expensive flight that day.

📐 Formation

AdjectiveComparativeSuperlative
1-syllable+ -er (cheaper)the + -est (cheapest)
2+ syllablesmore + adjthe most + adj
2-syllable -y+ -ier (happier)the -iest (happiest)
Past participlemore + adj (more tired)the most + adj
good / wellbetterbest
bad / badlyworseworst
farfarther / furtherfarthest / furthest
littlelessleast
many / muchmoremost

🔄 Other Ways of Comparing

  • as … as = equal: She is as tall as her brother.
  • not as … as = less: This is not as expensive as I thought.
  • less … than: This route is less crowded than the other.
  • the + comparative, the + comparative: The more you practise, the better you get.
  • much / far / a lot + comparative: She runs much faster than him.
  • a bit / a little + comparative: It's a bit colder today.
  • not as … as vs less … than are interchangeable in formal usage.
💡 Superlative + ever: It was the best film I've ever seen.
💡 as + adverb + as: She speaks as fluently as a native speaker.

📦 Nouns

Countable, uncountable, compound, group nouns and more

🔢 Countable vs Uncountable

CountableUncountable
Singular/Plurala ship / two shipswater (no plural)
Determinersa/an, many, few, severalsome, much, little, a lot of
Verb agreementThe ships are...The water is...
Both countable AND uncountable (different meanings):
paper (material, uncountable) ↔ a paper (newspaper/essay, countable)
glass (material) ↔ a glass (vessel)
experience (in general) ↔ an experience (particular event)

⚠️ Special Noun Types

  • Plural-only nouns — always plural, take plural verb: trousers, scissors, glasses, clothes, contents, outskirts, stairs
  • Singular-only nouns — look plural but take singular verb: news, mathematics, physics, economics, athletics, the United States
  • Pair nouns — things with two parts: a pair of scissors, a pair of trousers
  • Group/collective nouns — can be singular or plural: The team is / are playing tonight.

🔗 Compound Nouns

  • noun + noun: notebook, football, sunrise, coffee shop
  • adjective + noun: blackboard, greenhouse, quicksand
  • verb + noun: swimming pool, driving licence
  • noun + verb: sunrise, rainfall, heartbeat
The first noun usually describes/modifies the second. Stress is usually on the first element: 'coffee shop, 'car park, 'bedroom
💡 Từ uncountable phổ biến hay bị lỗi: advice, information, furniture, equipment, luggage, homework, knowledge, research, traffic, weather → KHÔNG dùng a/an hoặc thêm -s.

🔖 Articles

When to use the, a/an, or no article

📌 "THE" — Use when listener knows which one

  • with singular, plural or uncountable nouns when the listener can identify: Close the door.
  • when a following phrase/clause identifies it: the climate in this region
  • for unique things: the sun, the moon, the environment
  • superlatives: the most important problem
  • ordinals: the first time
  • musical instruments: play the piano

📌 "A / AN" — First mention or unspecified

  • first time mentioned: I saw a cat. The cat was black.
  • one of many (unspecified): He's a teacher.
  • describing/classifying: It's an interesting idea.
  • with jobs/titles (not specific): He's a doctor.
  • in number/quantity expressions: twice a week, a few, a lot

📌 NO ARTICLE — General / Generic

  • uncountable/plural nouns used generally: Water is essential. Dogs make good pets.
  • proper nouns (people, countries, continents): France, Asia, Maria
  • meals: have breakfast, cook dinner
  • languages, sports: speak English, play football
  • institutions (general purpose): at school, in hospital, go to church
  • transport: by car, on foot
  • names with "Lake, Mount": Lake Victoria, Mount Everest
💡 "in the future" vs "in future": In the future (later on) | In future (from now on, as a habit)

🔄 Reflexive Pronouns

  • Subject = Object: She hurt herself. / I taught myself English.
  • Emphasis: I cleaned the house myself. (= no one helped)
  • by myself/yourself = alone: He lives by himself.
  • Notice: each other (2 people) vs one another (3+)

⚙️ Special Uses of "it"

  • Weather/distance/time: It's raining. It's 5km. It's Monday.
  • Empty subject: It seems that he's wrong. It's easy to say.
  • Emphasis (cleft): It was Maria who called. It was on Monday that he left.

👥 You, We, They (General)

  • you/we — general "people": You never know what might happen.
  • they — authorities/people in general: They say this restaurant is excellent.

📎 its vs it's | my vs mine | a friend of mine

  • its = possessive: The cat licked its paw.
  • it's = it is / it has: It's raining.
  • my (+ noun) vs mine (standalone): My bag is heavy. This bag is mine.
  • a friend of mine = one of my friends (NOT a friend of me)

🕐 Present Simple

Facts, habits, routines, and general truths

🔧 Form

PositiveNegativeQuestion
I/you/we/theyworkdon't workDo you work?
he/she/itworksdoesn't workDoes she work?
Spelling: add -es after: ch, sh, ss, x, o → teaches, watches, goes
-y → -ies (after consonant): study → studies, carry → carries

📋 Uses

  • Habits / routines: She walks to work every day. (always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never)
  • Permanent facts / truths: The sun rises in the east.
  • General / scientific truths: Water boils at 100°C.
  • Timetables (future): The train leaves at 8am tomorrow.
  • Commentaries: He passes to Smith, who shoots!
  • Instructions: First you add the flour, then you mix.

🔄 Present Continuous

Actions happening now, temporary situations, and future arrangements

🔧 Form: am/is/are + verb-ing

I am working. | She is studying. | They are playing. | He isn't coming.

📋 Uses

  • Now / at this moment: She is reading a book right now.
  • Temporary situation: I am living in Hanoi for now. (not permanent)
  • Changing/developing situation: Prices are rising.
  • Future arrangements (personal plans): I am meeting her tomorrow.
  • Trends: More people are cycling to work nowadays.
  • Complaints (always): He is always losing his keys! (annoying habit)

⚠️ Stative Verbs — NOT used in continuous form

like, love, hate, want, need, prefer | know, believe, understand, remember, forget | have (possession), own, belong | seem, appear, look (= seem)
✗ I am knowing the answer. | ✓ I know the answer.
✗ She is having a car. | ✓ She has a car.
✓ She is having a good time. (= experiencing — action)
💡 Từ khóa: now, right now, at the moment, currently, these days, at present, this week, nowadays, look! listen!

⏪ Past Simple

Completed actions, states, and sequences in the past

🔧 Form

RegularIrregular
Positiveworkedwent / saw / came
Negativedidn't workdidn't go
QuestionDid you work?Did she go?
Spelling: stop → stopped | plan → planned | travel → travelled | study → studied

📋 Uses

  • Completed action at a specific time: She arrived at 6pm.

🌊 Past Continuous

Background actions and interrupted activities in the past

🔧 Form: was/were + verb-ing

I was sleeping. | She was studying. | They weren't listening. | Was he working?

📋 Uses

  • Background action interrupted by Past Simple: I was watching TV when she called.
  • Two simultaneous past actions: While she was cooking, he was setting the table.
  • Describing atmosphere/scene: The sun was shining. People were laughing.
  • Unfinished action at a past moment: At 9pm, they were still talking.
Past Continuous vs Past Simple:
I was reading a book. → in progress, background
I read the book. → completed action
💡 Từ khóa: while, when, as, at that moment, at 9 o'clock last night, all day yesterday, throughout the morning

✅ Present Perfect

Experiences, recent events, and past actions with present relevance

🔧 Form: have/has + past participle

I have worked. | She has arrived. | They haven't seen it. | Has he finished?

📋 Key Uses

  • just / already / yet:
    I've just arrived. (very recently, mid position)
    She's already eaten. (sooner than expected)
    Have you finished yet? / I haven't done it yet. (end, negative/question)
  • ever / never:
    Have you ever been to Japan? (in your life up to now)
    I've never eaten sushi. (not at any time in life)
  • for / since (duration to present):
    I've lived here for 3 years. (length of time)
    She hasn't called since Monday. (starting point)
  • recently / lately / in the last few days:
    I've been very busy lately.
    Recently, the government has changed its policy.
  • Superlative context:
    It's the best film I've ever seen.
💡 Present Perfect vs Past Simple:
Dùng PP khi thời gian KHÔNG xác định hoặc vẫn liên quan đến hiện tại.
Dùng Past Simple khi có thời gian CỤ THỂ đã qua.
✓ I have seen that film. (không biết khi nào)
✓ I saw that film last Tuesday. (thời gian cụ thể)

🔁 Present Perfect Continuous

Ongoing activities leading up to now, with visible results

🔧 Form: have/has + been + verb-ing

I have been working. | She has been studying all day. | Have you been waiting long?

📋 Uses & Contrast with Present Perfect

Present Perfect ContinuousPresent Perfect Simple
Activity in progress / recently stoppedCompleted action or result
How long / duration focusHow many / how much (quantity)
I've been painting the wall. (still wet)I've painted the wall. (finished)
She's been reading for 2 hours.She's read 3 books this month.
Temporary / recent activityChange/permanent result
Clues for continuous: tired, dirty, sweating, flushed — evidence of activity
Why are your hands dirty? — I've been gardening.
💡 Với "for" và "since": cả hai đều dùng được với PP Simple và PP Continuous.
PP Continuous nhấn mạnh quá trình; PP Simple nhấn mạnh kết quả.

📚 Past Perfect

The "past of the past" — what happened before another past moment

🔧 Form: had + past participle

She had left before I arrived. | They hadn't eaten. | Had you met him before?

📋 Uses

  • Action before another past action: When I got to the station, the train had already left.
  • Reporting past events (backshift): She said she had seen it before.
  • After expressions of time: After she had finished dinner, she went to bed.
  • Past Perfect Continuous: had + been + -ing
    She had been working for 8 hours before she collapsed. (duration before past moment)
Timeline: Event 1 (past perfect) ←→ Event 2 (past simple) ←→ NOW
By the time he arrived, we had already eaten.
💡 Từ khóa: by the time, after, before, when, already, just, never, as soon as

💫 Future Tenses

will, going to, Present Continuous, Future Perfect, and more

🔧 Future Forms Summary

FormUseExample
will + infinitivePrediction (no evidence), spontaneous decision, promise, offerI think it will rain. / I'll help you!
going to + infinitiveIntention/plan, prediction based on evidenceI'm going to study medicine. / Look — it's going to rain!
Present ContinuousFixed personal arrangementI am meeting Tom at 6pm.
Present SimpleTimetable/scheduleThe train leaves at 8am.
will be + -ingFuture continuous: in progress at a future timeAt 9pm, I will be sleeping.
will have + ppFuture perfect: completed before a future pointBy May, I will have finished.

🔍 will vs going to

  • will = spontaneous decision (deciding NOW): The phone's ringing — I'll get it!
  • going to = pre-decided intention: I'm going to buy a new laptop. I've already chosen one.
  • going to = evidence-based prediction: Look at those clouds! It's going to rain.
  • will = no evidence prediction / general prediction: I think the economy will improve.

⏰ shall / Future Perfect Continuous

  • shall (formal/British) in suggestions: Shall we go? / Shall I open the window?
  • will have been + -ing: By 2030, she will have been teaching for 20 years.
💡 Từ khóa: tomorrow, next week, soon, in the future, by the time, by next year, at 8pm tonight, this time next week, I think, I expect, probably
