5 Common Mistakes to Avoid
Sounding scripted: "My name is… I like…" — no personality, no natural chunks.
Translating word-by-word mentally → responses become slow, stiff, and unnatural.
Giving a one-sentence answer and stopping — no detail added, no follow-up question asked.
Not using connectors (because, so, but, lately, which is why) → sentences sound disconnected and choppy.
Flat delivery — no sentence stress, no natural rhythm or emotional variation.

5 Common Mistakes to Avoid
Ending your answer at a full stop — the exchange stalls completely with no momentum.
Hesitating too long — forgetting to use buy-time phrases such as "Let me think…"
Not responding to what the other person said — forgetting reactions such as "Really? / No way! / That sounds fun!"
Forgetting to ask back — "What about you?" should follow every response.
Pronouncing each word separately — not applying natural linking or elision in connected speech.

5 Common Mistakes to Avoid
Misplaced stress: stressing every word instead of punching content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives).
Overusing simple "I + V" patterns (I eat, I sleep, I study) — monotonous and lacking colour.
Storytelling without emotion — sounds like a report rather than a personal account.
Omitting time connectors → the narrative becomes fragmented and hard to follow.
Freezing when interrupted — not yet comfortable with fast English mode; practise the Chaos Drill more.
