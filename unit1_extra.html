Grammar

Present Simple & Continuous · State Verbs · Adverbs of Frequency

🔵 Present Simple PS

  • Facts / always true: My parents live in Poland.
  • Habits / routines: We study during the day.
  • State verbs: I love it!
  • Used with adverbs of frequency
⊕ do/does + V  |  ⊖ don't/doesn't + V  |  ? Do/Does + S + V?

🟠 Present Continuous PC

  • Happening right now: I'm studying design.
  • Temporary situation: I'm living with friends.
  • Keywords: now, at the moment, currently, these days, this week
⊕ am/is/are + V-ing

🚫 State Verbs SV

These verbs describe states, NOT actions. They are NOT usually used in continuous tenses.

Feelings/opinions: love, like, hate, prefer, want, need
Mental states: know, believe, understand, remember, forget
Others: belong, seem, own, be, have (possession)
✓ I love coffee.   ✗ I'm loving coffee.

📅 Adverbs of Frequency AF

Scale: always → usually → often → sometimes → occasionally → rarely / hardly ever → never

Expressions: every day, twice a week, once a month, once in a while, from time to time

Position: BEFORE main verb, AFTER be
I often see her.  |  She is always late.

Pronunciation

Connected Speech – do you → /dʒə/

📌 How "do you" sounds in natural speech

In fast / natural speech, "do you" is often reduced to /dʒə/ (sounds like the start of "jug").

Written: Do you cook for your family?
Spoken: /dʒə/ cook for your family?

This happens with all "do you" questions: What time do you start? How often do you see...?

Grammar

Modifiers – quite, fairly, pretty, relatively, a bit more/less, a lot more/less

🔵 Modifiers before adjectives

Use to show how much something is true:

quite + adj: quite normal
fairly + adj: fairly comfortable
pretty + adj: pretty normal
relatively + adj: relatively conservative
All four have similar meaning (less than "very")

🔵 quite a/an + adj + noun

Note: quite comes BEFORE a/an when followed by adj + noun:

✓ It's quite a difficult job.
✓ She's quite an easy-going person.
✗ It's a quite difficult job.

🟠 a bit more / a lot more

  • a bit more/less = a small amount more/less
  • a lot more/less = a large amount more/less
I'd like to travel a bit more. (small increase)
I want a lot more time with my family. (big increase)
I need to spend a lot less money. (big decrease)

🟠 Scale

not at all → a bit → fairly / quite / pretty / relatively → very → extremely
My life is a bit boring. (small amount)
My life is quite interesting. (moderate)
My life is very busy. (large amount)

📄 Model Text – Kim, games designer (UK)

Notice: modifiers · time expressions · present simple for routines

I'm a games designer and I live in Hackney in London. On a typical day, my alarm goes off at 6.00 a.m. and I try not to look at my emails or start work immediately. First, I do a bit of yoga and make a cup of green tea. I have a fairly healthy breakfast – some cereal with milk, or some toast, and then I cycle to work. It takes about twenty minutes and I find it really relaxing. At work, the first thing I do is switch on my computer and read my emails. For lunch, I get some noodles or something from one of the Vietnamese cafés or restaurants near to where I work. After work I like to meet up with friends for dinner, or go out somewhere. I love going to the cinema or the theatre or just staying at home listening to music.
