G1 · G2 · G3

Possessives · Verb "be" · Demonstratives

my / your / his / her / its / our / their + NOUN
Possessive adjectives always come BEFORE the noun.
My name is Linh. | Her brother is tall. | Their parents are teachers.
I am / am not  ·  She is / isn't  ·  We are / aren't
I am a student. | She is not tall. | We are Vietnamese.
This / That / These / Those
This is my sister. (near, singular) | These are my parents. (near, plural)
That is her husband. (far, singular) | Those are our cousins. (far, plural)

G9 · Past Tenses

Past Simple vs Used to

PAST SIMPLE — completed single event:
I grew up in a small town. | She passed away in 2015.
USED TO + base verb — past habit/state no longer true:
We used to visit our grandparents every weekend.
I used to be very shy. (but now I'm not)
💡 B1 fixed phrase: "I'll never forget the day…" — powerful for memorable events.

G11 · Relative Clauses

who / which / that / whose

WHO → replaces a person:
She married a man who she met at university.
WHICH → replaces a thing (non-defining, use commas):
The wedding, which was held in Hội An, was beautiful.
THAT → person or thing (defining, no commas):
It's a relationship that requires a lot of trust.
WHOSE → shows possession:
He's someone whose values align with mine.
⚡ B1+ NOTE: Non-defining clauses (with commas) CANNOT use "that".
