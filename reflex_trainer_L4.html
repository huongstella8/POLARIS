Must-Automate — Opinion & Connector Chunks
5 Common Mistakes to Avoid
Stating a bare fact with no feeling — "I like music" with no reason or emotion attached.
Skipping the connector — jumping to the next sentence without "because", "so", "honestly", or "which is why".
Using the same opener every time — vary between "I'd say", "Personally", "To be honest", "For me personally".
Giving an opinion with no ask-back — every opinion should end with "What about you?" or a follow-up question.
Flat delivery — opinion chunks need emphasis on the key feeling word, not read in a monotone.

Must-Automate — Personality Expressions
5 Common Mistakes to Avoid
Using only basic adjectives: shy, funny, nice — these tell nothing about your actual personality.
Describing yourself with extreme negatives — use hedges: "I'm kinda impatient sometimes" not "I'm very impatient".
Listing traits without examples — follow every trait with a natural example sentence.
Forgetting nuanced self-description — "I warm up slowly around new people" sounds far more authentic than "I'm shy".
No emotional tone — personality vocab needs conviction; the word "easygoing" should sound relaxed when said.

Must-Automate — Likes & Dislikes Range
5 Common Mistakes to Avoid
Using only "I like / I don't like" — the preference range is vast; use it.
Not qualifying the strength — "I kinda like" vs "I'm obsessed with" are very different; choose deliberately.
Stating dislike aggressively — "I hate" sounds harsh in conversation; use "I'm not really into" or "I can't stand" with a hedge.
No social collocations — "I prefer quiet places" is stronger with "I prefer quiet, low-key cafés honestly".
Forgetting to ask back — after every preference, invite the other person: "What about you?"

Must-Automate — Agreement & Reaction
5 Common Mistakes to Avoid
Only saying "Yes" or "No" — single-word responses shut conversations down immediately.
Agreeing with everything — natural conversation involves polite contrast; "Maybe, but…" shows engagement.
Disagreeing too directly — "You're wrong" sounds aggressive; use "I see your point, but…" or "Not necessarily."
No empathy expressions — when someone shares something difficult, react with "That must be stressful" before your opinion.
Freezing after a statement — use anti-freeze fillers: "Honestly…", "Let me think…", "For some reason…"
